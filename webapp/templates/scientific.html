fractional() - Convert to Fractions

Convert a floating-point number to a fraction representation.

Examples:
• 0.5 → "1/2"
• 1.5 → "1 1/2"
• 0.333 → "333/1000"
• 0.75 → "3/4"
Try: 0.5, 0.25, 0.333, 1.5, 2.75

Common Fractions Quick Reference:

0.5 = 1/2
0.25 = 1/4
0.75 = 3/4
0.333 ≈ 1/3
0.666 ≈ 2/3
0.125 = 1/8
0.2 = 1/5
0.6 = 3/5

scientific() - Scientific Notation

Return a number in scientific notation format with superscript exponents.

Examples:
• 500 → "5.00 x 10²"
• 0.003 → "3.00 x 10⁻³"
• 1234567 → "1.23 x 10⁶"
• 0.0000042 → "4.20 x 10⁻⁶"
Try: 500, 0.003, 1234567, 0.0000042, 6.022e23

Understanding Scientific Notation:

  • Positive exponents: Numbers greater than 10 (10² = 100)
  • Negative exponents: Numbers less than 1 (10⁻² = 0.01)
  • Zero exponent: Numbers between 1 and 10 (10⁰ = 1)
  • The mantissa (first number) is always between 1 and 10

Practice Examples

Fractions

  • 0.125 → 1/8
  • 0.875 → 7/8
  • 2.5 → 2 1/2
  • 3.75 → 3 3/4

Large Numbers

  • 1000 → 1.00 x 10³
  • 50000 → 5.00 x 10⁴
  • 1000000 → 1.00 x 10⁶
  • 7500000000 → 7.50 x 10⁹

Small Numbers

  • 0.01 → 1.00 x 10⁻²
  • 0.0001 → 1.00 x 10⁻⁴
  • 0.000001 → 1.00 x 10⁻⁶
  • 0.0000000001 → 1.00 x 10⁻¹⁰

Use Cases:

  • Fractions: Perfect for cooking measurements, music notation, and mathematical expressions
  • Scientific notation: Essential for representing very large numbers (astronomy, chemistry) or very small numbers (physics, nanotechnology)
  • Both formats make complex numbers easier to read and compare
  • Commonly used in scientific papers, engineering documents, and educational materials
